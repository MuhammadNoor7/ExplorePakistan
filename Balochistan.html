Princess Of Hope
Princess of Hope is a natural rock formation located in the Hingol National Park of Balochistan, Pakistan, resembling the profile of a princess and considered a significant landmark.
Rating: 4.8
$80per person

Princess Of Hope has geological origins dating back millions of years and gained cultural significance as a symbol of resilience and hope in the region.

Hazarganji-Chiltan National Park in Balochistan, Pakistan, is known for its rich biodiversity and is home to the endangered Chiltan wild goat.

Sapat Beach
Sapat Beach in Balochistan, Pakistan, is a pristine coastal stretch along the Arabian Sea, offering serene landscapes and a tranquil escape.
Rating: 4.2
$50 per person

The beach is home to a curious rock formation called Bujih Koh — a steep cliff rising from the sand entirely on its own. It’s separated from the cliff wall that stretches along the coast straddling the beach between its rocky embrace and the sea. The entire beach is home to a healthy colony of crabs.

Pir Ghaib
Pir Ghaib in Balochistan is a mystical and scenic gorge, renowned for its legend of a disappeared saint and its breathtaking natural beauty.
Rating: 5.0
$180 per person

A historical site associated with mysticism, believed to be the hiding place of a saint, with legends surrounding its mysterious disappearance and reappearance.
