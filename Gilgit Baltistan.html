Altit Fort
Altit Fort (Urdu: قلعہ التیت) is an ancient fort in the Altit town in the Hunza valley in Gilgit Baltistan, Pakistan. It was originally home to the hereditary rulers of the Hunza state who carried the title of 'Mir', although they moved to the somewhat younger Baltit fort nearby three centuries later.
Rating: 4.6
$55per person

Altit Fort is a tourist site which has been open to the public since 2007.

Attabad Lake
Attabad Lake (sometimes called Gojal Lake) is a lake in Hunza Valley in northern Pakistan. The lake was created by a landslide which happened in the village of Attabad on 4 January 2010.
Rating: 4.7
$40 per person

A look at the calm and clear blue water of Attabad Lake will make you feel like you are living in Heaven and the lake is really picturesque.

Rama Lake
Rama Lake is a lake close to Astore in Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan. It is on the pinnacle of the lovable Astore Valley that has a mild overlaying of very welltrees and different greenery.
Rating: 4.4

Sacred Stones of Hunza
The Sacred Rocks (Haldeikish) are situated between the Hunza River and the Karakorum Highway and a five minutes drive from the UNESCO enlisted village of Ganish, near the town of Karimabad in Hunza Valley.
Rating: 4.3
$29 per person

The Sacred Rocks of Hunza or Haldeikish is one of the earliest sites of Petroglyphs along the ancient Silk Route. It is a cultural heritage site in Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan.
